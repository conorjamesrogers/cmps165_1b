Project Title
  • Approximate Orbital Display
Purpose or Objective
  • To visualize an approximation of satellites orbiting the earth. A 3D display of earth that will be zoomable and show a position of satellites orbiting the planet. The Data needed is retreivable online-- Using the US airforce's "Space-Track.org" we can retrieve TLE (Two Line Element) of satellite bodies and cross reference the satellite number with other data. A JS library (satellite.js) will decipher and do all the heavy lifting-- allowing us to impliment the front end. After getting the inclination ect. we can display the satellites “orbit” and display some simple movement in real time. The Airforce has A L O T of TLEs, therefore I will probably only include some of the latest Satellites launched.
Data Sources
  • UCSUSA provides a .csv full of satellite information
  • Space-Track A US Airforce database of TLE's of all non-classified satellites
  • Heavens Above Database Used in last years Satellite Flares project. Essencialy uses Space-Track and TLEs-- can be used to double check outputs.
